Chapter 1 · The Problem

Today's infection tests only catch a fight.

  • ESR, CRP, synovial WBC — all measure one thing: the neutrophil response.
  • No neutrophils, no diagnosis — even when bacteria are still there.
  • Biofilm bacteria specialize in not provoking neutrophils.
  • Persisters and small-colony variants survive antibiotics and dodge phagocytosis.

Chapter 2 · The Finding

The "cleared" joints looked just like the infected ones.

  • 30 patients · synovial fluid + plasma · 96 proteins (Olink).
  • 3 groups: 7 active, 11 MSIS-cleared after prior infection, 12 aseptic.
  • Unsupervised clustering: prior infections grouped with active, not aseptic.
  • Plasma could not tell the groups apart — the signal lives in the joint.

Chapter 4 · The Mechanism

The same trick cancer uses — and a new map.

  • Up: wound healing, viral-process, granulocyte activation programs.
  • Down: T-cell selection and proliferation.
  • ANGPT1/TIE2, PDGF-B, EGF, NOS3 stabilize the endothelium → neutrophils stay in the bloodstream.
  • Peripheral immune tolerance — borrowed from chronic viruses and solid tumors.

The cancer parallel

  • Cancer stem cells build immunosuppressive microenvironments to evade clearance. Biofilm bacteria appear to do the same.
  • CXCL5 in tumors activates CXCR2, recruits pro-tumor immune cells, and drives angiogenesis — high CXCL5 predicts worse survival in glioblastoma.
  • In dormant joints, the same CXCL5-anchored circuit may help bacteria remodel tissue into a survival niche.
  • Galectin-1 ↓ in dormant joints mirrors the immune-suppressive lectin axis exploited by many tumors.
  • Pathways up-regulated in dormancy (negative regulation of cytokine production, endothelial apoptosis, immune response) read like a tumor microenvironment signature.
  • Therapeutic implication: immune-checkpoint and chemokine-axis drugs developed for oncology (PD-L1 inhibitors, CXCL5/CXCR2 antagonists like SB225002 / AZD-8309, ANGPT1/TIE2 modulators like AKB-9778) may be repurposable for dormant infection.
An immune truce between host and microbe — neither eradicating the bacteria nor allowing full inflammatory resolution.— from the Discussion
