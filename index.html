Derek F. Amanatullah, M.D., Ph.D.

Surgeon · Researcher · Innovator · Leader

I bring together engineering, science, surgery, and entrepreneurship to improve the lives of patients undergoing joint replacement surgery. I am an Associate Professor of Orthopaedic Surgery at Stanford University and the co-Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of Bone and Joint Infection. I trained as a biomedical engineer at USC, a physician-scientist at Albert Einstein, an orthopaedic surgeon at UC Davis, and an arthroplasty fellow at Mayo Clinic. I am board-certified by the American Board of Orthopaedic Surgery and a member of the living Hall-of-Fame of knee replacement surgery (known as the closed Knee Society). My work spans unmasking hidden infections to building AI tools that make the operating room safer. I believe the best ideas start at the bedside, and I am driven by those ideas to make care better for every patient.
